Jonathan Middlemiss

Jonathan Middlemiss was a renowned artist known for his exceptional skills in creating intricate ceramic sculptures which integrate themes of geometry, ecology, and landscape. His art is inspired by nature, and he often used natural materials such as wood, stone, and metal in his works. He was born in 1949 and passed away in 2021, leaving behind an impressive collection of art that continues to inspire and captivate art enthusiasts worldwide.

The day before his passing he gifted all images and rights of his lifetime’s work to his daughter, with the instructions to use them and say “My Dad did that”. Jonathan always said that the art is his children’s legacy, Serena is committed to preserving this legacy with his life’s philosophy that inspired new perspectives in art and personal transformation. With a background in design and a lifetime of understanding her father's work, Serena is the one person suited to organizing exhibitions, authenticating, and promoting her father's artwork. She currently cataloguing and documenting her father's extensive collection of art and writing his biography to ensure that his complete story is preserved and accessible to future generations.

This extensive knowledge of her father's work has allowed her to continue his legacy in a meaningful way as she understands the themes and techniques that her father used, and has a keen eye for identifying his artwork. In addition to her knowledge of her father's work, Serena also has a wealth of experience and interest in art, design and technology.

Jonathan Middlemiss was a talented artist who left behind a remarkable legacy. His art continues to inspire and captivate audiences worldwide. We would very much like to hear from individual collectors, galleries and other people who had an interest in his life and work.
